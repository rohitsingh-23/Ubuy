Description

Style:Wireless

SmartHop Technology ASUS SmartHop technology scans the radio spectrum a thousand times per second, identifying congestion and interference around the 2.4 GHz band. It allows ROG Strix Impact II Wireless to seamlessly switch between channels to ensure strong, stable connections — regardless of the other wireless devices nearby. Unmatched Precision ROG collaborated with professional gamers in developing ROG Strix Impact II Wireless. The result is an optimized, ergonomic gaming mouse that weighs just 93 grams. It features a 16,000 dpi sensor that tracks up to 400 inches per second, with a maximum acceleration of up to 40 G, so you're assured of superfast responses and precision control – all without a hint of lag. Long-Lasting Battery Life Play wirelessly for up to an amazing 89 hours with RGB lighting off, or up to 48 hours with lights on. Five Programmable Buttons All five buttons are configurable, so you can execute macros, favorite functions or complex moves with a single click. Three Onboard Memory Slots Save sensitivity level, lighting or other settings and store them in the onboard memory, so you're ready for action wherever you go. Exclusive Push-Fit Switch Socket Design Push-fit switch sockets make it easy to swap out the switches in ROG Strix Impact II Wireless, giving you the flexibility to install your preferred switches to tailor actuation force and feedback. You can also replace worn or broken switches to extend the lifespan of the mouse. Pivoted Button Mechanism Shorter travel means quicker clicks, which is why our engineers equipped ROG Strix Impact II Wireless with pivoted buttons. The mechanism utilizes springs and metal hinges to balance the keys and minimize the distance between the buttons and switches. The upshot is minimal travel and rapid return, ensuring superb speed, responsiveness and consistency with every click. On-the-fly DPI Button An integrated DPI button lets you flip quickly between four different sensitivity settings or three customized profiles, on the fly. As an added bonus, the Aura RGB illumination changes color to reflect your choice, providing an instant visual cue when you land on the desired sensitivity. Note: DPI sensitivity level can also be set in Armoury Crate. Aura Sync RGB Lighting ROG Strix Impact II Wireless features Aura RGB lighting integrated into the logo and scroll wheel to illuminate your battlefield. Lighting can be customized with a near-endless spectrum of colors and a range of dynamic effects, and up to three profiles can be stored onboard, so your preferences travel with you. Armoury Crate Armoury Crate is an enhanced driver-based, peripherals-configuration utility that offers extensive controls. An intuitive UI lets you easily tune ROG Strix Impact II Wireless to suit your gameplay – adjust performance and surface calibration settings, program and map buttons, customize lighting effects, and more. You can even track hardware stats during gameplay for data analysis.

Great quality gaming mouse!

The ASUS ROG Strix Impact II Wireless Gaming mouse is hands down about one of the best gaming and content creation mice I have ever used. The PTFE/Teflon feet glide across my desk and mousepad like greased lightning! I've never had a mouse this smooth. I'm coming from Corsair's Dark Core v1 AND v2 wireless gaming mice and in comparison, they just feel so awkward and clumsy compared the the ASUS ROG Strix Impact II. With The Dark Core v1 AND v2 mice, I would have a difficult time when clicking buttons and getting the clicks to register. My hands are a bit smaller and no matter what Corsair says, it's not geared for smaller hands and does not track well. On the other hand, ( pun intended :) ), The ASUS ROG Strix Impact II wireless gaming mouse reads my mouse clicks from any point when pressing the buttons. If you decided that you want to run the mouse wired, the wire is very easy to work with and manipulates very well when in motion while attached to the mouse. The RGB are bright, but not overwhelming and the colors are true. The mouse wheel doesn't require extra force to get it to spin and it rolls perfectly balanced between the amount of force required to rotate the wheel, texture and grip of the wheel and the actual steps of the wheel. While in game, I have never encountered an issue at all. the only reason I dropped 1 star off the review is because I have noticed that every once in a while I do experience either a bit of lag or a sensor problem that causes the mouse to pointer to jump or skip. It's very rare but noticeable when it happens. All in all, I would highly recommend this mouse over anything Corsair has to offer, or any other brand for that matter.
